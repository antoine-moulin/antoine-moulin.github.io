Antoine Moulin

Hi! I am a second-year PhD student in reinforcement learning and as part of the ELLIS PhD program, I am co-supervised by Gergely Neu and Arthur Gretton. My main objective during this PhD is to gain a deeper understanding of the challenges posed by large-scale reinforcement learning by identifying and exploiting structural properties of Markov decision processes (MDPs) that make large-scale learning statistically and computationally feasible.

At the moment, I am particularly interested in efficiently solving the exploration-exploitation tradeoff in infinite-horizon MDPs under (linear) function approximation. My research interests also include: online learning, offline reinforcement learning, representation learning.
